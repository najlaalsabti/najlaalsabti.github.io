This seems like a stroke.
if the patient also had any of:

  • No balance
  • Trouble seeing
  • Trouble raising both arms

Do the following:

  • Call 911 immediately
  • Note the time you first see symptoms
  • Perform CPR, if necessary
  • Do not let that person go to sleep
    or talk you out of calling 911
  • Do not give them medication,
    food, or drinks
  • Do not drive to the emergency room

This seems like a seizure.
if the patient also had any of:

  • Sudden falls
  • Confusion
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Uncontrollable jerking movements
  • Strange nations and emotions

Do the following:

  • Turn patient on side and start
    timing the seizure
  • Stay with patient, do no restrain
  • Place something soft under the head
  • Do NOT put anything in mouth
  • Call 911 if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes

This seems like an internal bleeding.
if the patient also had any of:

  • Weakness
  • Vision changes
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest or abdominal pain
  • Tingling hands or feet

Do the following:

  • Lie the patient down
  • Cover them to keep them warm
  • Raise the patient’s legs above their heart
  • Don’t give them anything to eat or drink
  • If the patient becomes unconscious,
    place them on their side
  • Begin (CPR) if necessary
